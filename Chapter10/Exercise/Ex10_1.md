### Exercise 10.1

A force of
\[
\begin{bmatrix}
1 \\
2 \\
3
\end{bmatrix}
\]
is applied to an object at a point
\[
\begin{bmatrix}
1 \\
-1 \\
0
\end{bmatrix}
\]
relative to its center of mass. What is the torque generated by this force?

### Given:

- Force vector $\mathbf{F} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 2 \\ 3 \end{bmatrix}$
- Position vector $\mathbf{r} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -1 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix}$

The torque $\mathbf{\tau}$ generated by a force applied at a point relative to the center of mass is given by the cross product:
$$
\mathbf{\tau} = \mathbf{r} \times \mathbf{F}
$$

### Step 1: Write the cross product formula

The cross product of two vectors $\mathbf{r} = (r_1, r_2, r_3)$ and $\mathbf{F} = (F_1, F_2, F_3)$ in 3D space is calculated as:
$$
\mathbf{r} \times \mathbf{F} = \begin{vmatrix}
\mathbf{i} & \mathbf{j} & \mathbf{k} \\
r_1 & r_2 & r_3 \\
F_1 & F_2 & F_3
\end{vmatrix}
$$
Substitute the values of $\mathbf{r} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -1 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix}$ and $\mathbf{F} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 2 \\ 3 \end{bmatrix}$ into the determinant:
$$
\mathbf{r} \times \mathbf{F} = \begin{vmatrix}
\mathbf{i} & \mathbf{j} & \mathbf{k} \\
1 & -1 & 0 \\
1 & 2 & 3
\end{vmatrix}
$$

### Step 2: Expand the determinant

Now, expand the determinant:
$$
\mathbf{r} \times \mathbf{F} = \mathbf{i} \begin{vmatrix} -1 & 0 \\ 2 & 3 \end{vmatrix} - \mathbf{j} \begin{vmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 1 & 3 \end{vmatrix} + \mathbf{k} \begin{vmatrix} 1 & -1 \\ 1 & 2 \end{vmatrix}
$$

### Step 3: Calculate the 2x2 determinants

Now, compute each of the 2x2 determinants:

- For $\mathbf{i}$: $\begin{vmatrix} -1 & 0 \\ 2 & 3 \end{vmatrix} = (-1)(3) - (0)(2) = -3$
- For $\mathbf{j}$: $\begin{vmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 1 & 3 \end{vmatrix} = (1)(3) - (0)(1) = 3$
- For $\mathbf{k}$: $\begin{vmatrix} 1 & -1 \\ 1 & 2 \end{vmatrix} = (1)(2) - (-1)(1) = 2 + 1 = 3$

### Step 4: Substitute the results

Now, substitute the values back into the original equation:
$$
\mathbf{r} \times \mathbf{F} = \mathbf{i}(-3) - \mathbf{j}(3) + \mathbf{k}(3)
$$
This simplifies to:
$$
\mathbf{r} \times \mathbf{F} = -3 \mathbf{i} - 3 \mathbf{j} + 3 \mathbf{k}
$$

### Final Answer:

Therefore, the torque $\mathbf{\tau}$ generated by the force is:
$$
\mathbf{\tau} = \begin{bmatrix} -3 \\ -3 \\ 3 \end{bmatrix}
$$
This is the torque vector generated by the given force applied at the given position.
